Old-style slot machines have mechanical coin acceptors that need to be manually set up to accept the correct coin denominations. If you own an old-style slot machine and need to set up the coin acceptor, follow these steps:

Step 1: Gather Necessary Tools

  • Screwdriver
  • Needle-nose pliers

Step 2: Remove the Coin Mechanism

  • Turn off the machine and unplug it.
  • Remove the front panel of the machine.
  • Locate the coin mechanism and remove it by unscrewing it from the inside of the machine.

Step 3: Identify the Coin Denominations

  • Look for the coin denomination stickers on the coin mechanism or on the front of the machine.
  • Use needle-nose pliers to remove any unwanted stickers.

Step 4: Adjust the Coin Mechanism

  • Use the screwdriver to adjust the coin mechanism to accept the correct coins.
  • Adjust the coin mechanism until it can accept the correct coins without jamming or rejecting them.

Step 5: Test the Coin Mechanism

  • Reinstall the coin mechanism back into the machine.
  • Plug in the machine and turn it on.
  • Test the coin mechanism by inserting each accepted coin denomination into the machine and ensure that it registers on the machine’s display.

With these steps, you should be able to set up the coin acceptor of your old-style slot machine to accept the correct coin denominations. Always follow safety precautions when working on your machine and never attempt to modify or alter the machine’s wiring or electrical components.
